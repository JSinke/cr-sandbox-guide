Module 1 — Memberships
Create 4 membership types (Non-Member, paid Individual, Family Standard, Family Premium)
KB sourced
KB sources: Create Membership Types · Membership Type – General · Membership Type – Price · Create a Non-Member Membership · Create Family Membership Types
Navigate to Memberships
1
In the left nav, click MEMBERSHIPS. You'll see two tabs: Individual and Family.
KB: "Each registered person on the platform must have a membership type. System users can create and assign membership types to grant club-specific platform permissions, restrictions, and fees."
Task 1 of 4 — Non-Member (Individual, free)
1
On the Individual tab, click the green Create button. Name: Non-Member. Short Code optional. Click Save.
2
On the General tab: confirm Is Active is checked. Optionally check Make This Membership Default For Members.
KB: "If the box is unchecked, this membership is not available and will be hidden from both the member portal and the admin panel view."
3
Click the Membership Price tab. Leave Requires Fee? unchecked — this is a free membership.
KB: "A basic Non-Member membership typically does not include a membership fee, so the Requires Fee? option is left unchecked."
4
On the Reservation Rules tab, leave court booking enabled for sandbox testing. On the Portal Settings tab, check Is Public. Click Save Changes.
Task 2 of 4 — Paid Individual (your choice of name)
1
Click Create again. Name it something like Gold Member. Click Save.
2
On Membership Price, check Requires Fee?. Under Frequency, check Monthly and enter $50. You can enable multiple frequencies so members can choose.
KB: "For a single membership type, you can enable one or multiple frequency options, allowing players to choose the option that best suits them."
3
Optionally set X Days Past Due to Expire Account (grace period) and X Days Past Due to Auto-Cancel. Click Save Changes.
KB: "If set to 0 days, memberships expire immediately at midnight after the renewal date if payment hasn't been received. Both settings are calculated from the original due date, not from each other."
Task 3 of 4 — Family Standard (monthly)
1
Click the Family tab > Create Family Membership. Name: Family Standard. Save.
2
On General, set Max Family Members (e.g. 4).
KB: "When a system user creates a Family membership type, CourtReserve shows an additional field: Max Family Members."
3
On Membership Price: check Requires Fee?, set frequency to Monthly at $80/month. Click Save Changes.
Task 4 of 4 — Family Premium (annual + proration)
1
Create another Family Membership named Family Premium. On Membership Price: enable Yearly at $800/year.
2
Enable Prorate Membership on Member Portal so all members align to the same billing cycle.
KB example: "A new member joins October 27 at $50/month. With proration, the system charges $8.06 for Oct 27–31 ($1.61/day), and the next full payment is November 1st."
3
Click Save Changes. Verify all 4 memberships appear in Reports > Membership Status Report.

Module 2 — General Settings
Organization settings, hours of operation, calendar feed, two-factor authentication
KB sourced
KB sources: Organization Settings: General · Hours of Operation · CourtReserve Calendar Feed · Two-Factor Authentication
Review General Settings — know every field
1
Go to Settings > Organization Settings row > click the General icon. Read every field on this page.
2
Key fields: Time Zone (all scheduling), Reservation Min Interval (time block size on scheduler — e.g. 60 min = slots every hour), Default Scheduler (Expanded is standard unless auto-assigning courts).
KB: "These settings lay the foundation for how your club operates within CourtReserve — from hours of operation to reservation rules and email preferences."
3
Note the Send Event/Reservation Reminders toggles and the X Hours Prior fields — admins control how far in advance reminder emails fire. Don't change anything yet, just read and understand.
Set Hours of Operation
1
Go to Settings > Organization Settings row > click Hours of Operation. Set Open = 9:00 AM, Close = 9:00 PM on Monday.
2
Click Mark other days as current day to copy those hours to all 7 days at once.
KB: "If the hours are the same each day, use the Mark other days as current day button to copy the current day's hours to all other days."
3
Leave Update Court Schedules checked (it's the default). This auto-updates all individual court schedules to match. Click Save.
KB: "If Update Court Schedules is selected (the default setting), the daily court schedules will automatically update to reflect changes made to the Hours of Operation."
Get Your Calendar Feed Link
1
Open the member portal > click your name in the top-right corner > click Calendar Feed. Select Copy Link.
KB: "The easiest way to add your CourtReserve calendar feed is to copy the link to your clipboard by selecting Copy Link."
2
This URL lets members subscribe in Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook so the club's schedule auto-syncs to their personal calendar.
Enable Two-Factor Authentication
1
Go to Settings > Organization Settings row > click Two-Factor Authentication. Click to enable.
KB: "This requires system users to verify their identity by entering a code sent to their email after successfully entering their login credentials."
2
A 6-digit code is sent to your admin email. Enter it to confirm activation. Log out and back in — the 2FA prompt will appear.
3
To disable later: return to the same page and enter another emailed verification code.

Module 3 — System Users
Create Admin, Sub-Admin, and Event Coordinator accounts; compare role permissions
KB sourced
KB sources: Creating and Managing System Users (collection)
Understand the 3 Role Types
1
Admin — Full access to all settings, reports, billing, and features. Best for owners and senior managers.
2
Sub-Admin — Customizable limited access. The specific permissions can be toggled per sub-admin. Best for front desk staff.
3
Event Coordinator — Access limited to event management only. Best for program directors who don't need financial or court access.
Create One of Each User Type
1
Go to Settings > System Users > click Add User.
2
Create User 1 (Admin): test name and email, Role = Admin. Save.
3
Create User 2 (Sub-Admin): different test name and email, Role = Sub-Admin. Explore the permissions checkboxes — note which features are individually togglable. Save.
4
Create User 3 (Event Coordinator): another test name and email, Role = Event Coordinator. Save.
5
Log out and log in as each user type. Navigate the admin panel and observe what each role can and cannot see. This is high-value prep — customers ask about permission differences on nearly every onboarding call.

Edit Tennis Court 1. Scroll to the Combo Court(s) field. Select Pickleball Court 1 and Pickleball Court 2. Save.

Create Ball Machines (Resources)
1
Go to Settings > COURT & RESOURCE SETTINGS > Manage Resources > Create New.
KB: "A resource is any item a member can book in addition to — or instead of — a court, such as a ball machine, TV, projector, or other equipment."
2
Select Resource Category = Ball Machine. Name = Ball Machine 1. Confirm Is Active. Save. Repeat for Ball Machine 2 and Ball Machine 3.
Create 2 Closures
1
Confirm closure types exist: Settings > COURT & RESOURCE SETTINGS > Closure Types. Create Weather and Maintenance if missing.
KB: "A closure type must be created and available to assign to closures."
2
Go to Closures > Create Closure. Closure 1 (Weather): type = Weather, tomorrow 12–2pm, all pickleball courts, Cancellation Policy = No Cancellations. Confirm.

3
Closure 2 (Recurring Maintenance): type = Maintenance, 7:00–8:00am, Recurrence = Weekly on Monday, all courts. Confirm.
KB: "System users can create a recurring closure if the closure starts and finishes in one calendar day. It is not possible to create a recurring closure that starts on one day and finishes on another."

Module 5 — Custom Schedulers
Create 3 schedulers: Pickleball, Tennis, Conference Rooms
KB sourced
KB sources: Overview: Custom Schedulers
Understand Custom Schedulers
1
Custom Schedulers filter the default all-courts view to show only specific courts — and optionally only to specific membership types or member groups.
KB: "Custom Schedulers are fully customizable booking calendars that use filters to control what is displayed and who can see it." Important: "The custom scheduler(s) must be built within your standard hours of operation."
2
Navigate: Settings > COURT & BALL MACHINE SETTINGS heading > click Custom Schedulers > Create Custom Scheduler.
Create Scheduler 1 — Pickleball
1
Scheduler name: Pickleball. Navigation name in Member Portal: Book Pickleball. Check the portal name checkbox.
KB: "CourtReserve shows this name on the member portal if you enable this feature with the adjacent checkbox."
2
Scheduler Type: Expanded (members pick their own court). Court Types: Pickleball. Courts: select Courts 1–4. Show on Member Portal: checked. Show on Admin Side: checked. Save.
KB: "If you are not auto-assigning courts, choose Expanded."
Create Scheduler 2 — Tennis & Scheduler 3 — Conference Rooms
1
Tennis: name = Tennis, portal name = Book Tennis, Type = Expanded, Courts = Tennis Court 1 & 2. Show on portal and admin. Save.
2
Conference Rooms: name = Conference Rooms, portal name = Book a Room, Type = Expanded, Courts = Conference Room. Show on portal and admin. Save.
3
View the member portal. You should now see Book Pickleball, Book Tennis, and Book a Room as three separate navigation options. Click each and confirm only the correct courts appear.

Module 6 — Reservations & Booking Settings
Create 8 reservation types + configure booking settings, court opening times, and prime times
KB sourced
KB sources: Create Reservation Types · Booking Settings: General · Court Opening Times · Prime Times
Navigate to Reservation Types
1
Go to Settings > RESERVATION SETTINGS row > click Reservation Types. Click the green Create Reservation Type button.
2
Key fields: Min/Max Reservation Time in Minutes (booking range), Default Duration (pre-filled when member books), Duration Increment (how much time added per click), Min/Max Players (headcount enforcement).
KB example: Singles – Tennis with Min 30, Max 120, Default 60, Increment 30 means members can book from 30 min to 2 hrs in 30-min steps, pre-filled at 1 hour.
Create Pickleball Types
1
Pickleball Singles: Assign to pickleball courts. Min/Max players = 2. Min 60, Max 120, Default 60, Increment 30. Guests: Yes ($10/hr).
2
Pickleball Doubles: Min/Max players = 4–5. Min 90, Max 120, Default 90, Increment 30. Guests: Yes ($5/hr).
3
Drilling: Min/Max players = 1–2. Min/Max/Default all 30 min (fixed-length). Guests: No.
Create Tennis, Pool & Contract Types
1
Tennis Singles: Tennis courts, 2 players, Min 30/Max 90/Default 60/Increment 30. Guests free.
2
Tennis Doubles: Tennis courts, 4 players, Min 60/Max 150/Default 90/Increment 30. Guests free.
3
Pool Reservation: Assign pool court, no player restrictions, Min 30/Max 180/Increment 30. Guests: No.
4
Contract Time: No restrictions. Set Allow Members to Book Online to OFF — this becomes an admin-only reservation type.
KB: "If disabled, users can see the scheduler but cannot book a court or resource." This is how to create admin-only reservation types.
Configure Court Opening Times
1
Go to Settings > BOOKING SETTINGS heading > click General. Find Time to Open Next Available Reservation Day. Set to 9:15 AM.
KB: "If a Time to Open Next Available Reservation Day is set, the entirety of the latest day available for booking will open at the specified time, allowing for more structured and predictable reservation planning."
2
Set Time in Advance a Court Can Be Booked to 14 days for your Gold membership type. Other types can have different limits.
Configure Prime Times
1
Go to Settings > BOOKING SETTINGS > click Prime Times. Click the + icon next to Saturday. Set 9:00 AM – 12:00 PM.
KB: "There is no Save button on this page. CourtReserve auto-saves any changes." Also: "Prime time settings manage access — they are not connected to pricing or custom cost settings."
2
Go to Booking Settings > Restrictions to configure which membership types are blocked from booking during prime time hours.

Setup Order — Must Complete in This Order
1
Navigate to Settings > INSTRUCTOR SETTINGS heading. You will see icons for: Instructor Types, Lesson Types, Instructors, Instructor Pricing, and Book a Pro Setup.
KB: "Before adding an instructor, make sure the following are set up first: Instructor Types, Lesson Types, Event Categories, Instructor Availability."

Click Instructor Types > Create Instructor Type. Create: "Tennis Pro" and "Pickleball Pro". Save each.
3
Click Lesson Types > Create Lesson Type. Create: Private, Pro+2, Pro+3. Set Min/Max players and whether a court is required for each.
KB: "A Lesson Type is a high-level tag that defines and categorizes lessons — including basic details, court settings, appearance, and financial settings."
Create Instructors
1
Go to Instructors icon > Create Instructor. Fill in: First Name, Last Name, Email (required). Check Is Instructor? to reveal instructor-specific fields.
KB: "The Create Instructor feature lets system users build instructor profiles including availability, permissions, lesson/event types, and scheduling rules."
2
Assign Instructor Type (e.g., Tennis Pro). Assign which Lesson Types they can teach. Save. Create at least 1 tennis pro and 1 pickleball pro.
3
For the second instructor, use Copy Settings From Other Users to duplicate the first profile, then modify only what's different — saves significant time.
Set Instructor Pricing & Book A Pro
1
Click Instructor Pricing. Set base lesson rate: $60/hour for both instructors. Set Pro+2 group rate: $35/person for 60 min.
2
Click Book a Pro Setup > Create Book a Pro Scheduler. Select the instructor, lesson types, available times, and courts. Save.

3
Create one Book a Pro scheduler per instructor. View the member portal to confirm the "Book a Pro" section appears with each instructor's availability.

Method 1 — Member Import (Know the Rules)
1
Read the Member List Import KB article. Download the Excel template. Memorize the import rules.

1
Go to Settings > PORTAL SETTINGS row > click Sign-Up Form.

Understand Widgets
1
Widgets are embeddable tools clubs paste into their own website so members can book courts, register for events, or sign up without leaving the club's site.
KB: "Widgets are designed to run in a web browser. System users can copy and paste a widget link onto their website or display it on a front desk smart TV."
2
Visit real examples: The Kitchen PB, Lucky Shots PB, USTA National Campus. Identify which widget types each club is using.
Create 3 Widgets
1
Navigate: Settings > scroll to WIDGET row. Click the widget type icon you want to create.
2
Widget 1 — Court Scheduler: Type = Courts Scheduler. Configure which courts to show and membership types allowed. Save. Copy the generated embed code and paste into a blank HTML file on your computer to see how it renders.
3
Widget 2 — Events: Type = Events Calendar or Events List. Configure event categories, date range, and style. Save and copy embed code.
4
Widget 3 — Member Registration: Type = Member Registration. This embeds the sign-up form on any external website. Save and copy embed code. This is what a web developer would paste into the club's WordPress or Squarespace site.
Bonus — Organized Play Setup
1
Enable: Settings > ADDITIONAL FEATURES > Additional Features > scroll to Enhanced Play > check Organized Play. Click Setup.
KB: "Organized Play is a dynamic and competitive format where players are matched using system-generated pairings based on their ratings. Admins can organize matches across multiple courts without manual scheduling."
2
After enabling, a standalone Organized Play icon appears in the ADDITIONAL FEATURES row. Click it > configure your first event: set the courts, rating range, number of rounds, and registration window.
3
As a test member, register for the Organized Play event. As admin, generate pairings. View the match schedule from both admin and member portal perspectives.
