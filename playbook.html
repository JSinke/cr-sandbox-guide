📥
Triage & Ticket Workflow
How tickets move from inbox to resolution — weekends, early morning, and daily flow
Must Know
Weekend Triage
Customers receive an automated message confirming their ticket is in the queue. Tickets enter the Triage Inbox first. If solvable within 2–3 minutes — answer immediately. If not — ask clarifying questions, wait for info, then assign to Customer Success Inbox via Round Robin.
Early Morning
1. Log into Intercom → go to the Customer Success Inbox.
2. Filter from oldest to newest.
3. Move any tickets in Triage or Unassigned into the CS Inbox.
4. Handle ⭐ priority star tickets immediately — these are bigger clients.
5. Answer and snooze tickets you can solve right away to clear the box.
Technical Support Inbox
Assign tickets here when you cannot resolve on your own. Before doing so:
• Research thoroughly using KB, Fin AI, and Slack
• Ask for guidance in #cs-questions (include the ticket URL)
• Summarize your research at the bottom of the ticket
• Use the Escalation Tag Macro to ensure all summary info is included

The Technical Support Specialist (currently Oswin) will either tag it as a bug or return it with the correct info.

Intercom Rules & Snoozing
Snoozing rules, round robin, inbox cap, duplicate conversations, AI tagging
Must Know
Snoozing Rules
Snoozing does NOT impact SLA reporting. Only snooze a ticket when you have resolved the issue and are waiting for a customer response. Do not snooze unresolved tickets — this leads to overloaded inboxes.
Round Robin & Inbox Cap
We use Balance Assign with Round Robin, capping inboxes at a maximum of 5 active tickets.
AI Tagging Protocol
When Rally (AI chatbot) gives an incorrect response:
1. Tag the conversation with "Incorrect AI" in Intercom
2. Add a brief internal note explaining why it was incorrect
3. Post in #tsa-requests Slack channel
4. Respond to the customer with the correct guidance
Duplicate Conversations
If an admin opens multiple tickets: notify them, consolidate into one conversation, copy pertinent info from duplicates. Use Intercom's Merge Conversation feature when appropriate.
Co-Pilot in Intercom
Everyone should have Fin AI turned on in their Intercom account. Review the Co-Pilot guide for usage best practices.

CS Questions Channel
How to use #cs-questions, emoji protocols, and special routing rules
Important
How It Works
CS Questions is for tickets you need help with or cannot resolve. Always use CS Questions instead of mentions. Everyone is expected to actively participate throughout the day.
Emoji Protocol
• 👀 = "I'm working on this" — prevents duplicate effort
• ✅ = "This has been resolved"
Subscription / Billing Questions
If a customer asks about subscription billing or declined payments — post in CSQ and tag Jennifer Conrad directly.
Always include the ticket URL when posting in CS Questions. Without it, teammates cannot help efficiently.

Player vs. Admin Handling
What to do when a player contacts support, password resets, and suspicious emails
Must Know
When a Player Contacts Intercom
Default: redirect them to their organization. Most player issues (can't book, can't access org, can't see an event) are org issues — not system issues. Only assist players if:
• They demonstrate a system-wide issue
• Their issue spans multiple orgs and wasn't caused by user error
• The question is simple enough to answer quickly (e.g., Sendgrid check)
Password Resets
Do NOT reset passwords for anyone. Direct all password reset requests — admin or player — to: app.courtreserve.com/Account/ForgotPassword
Admins Emailing from Non-Org Emails
Do not provide org details to an email not associated with that org's admins. Ask for their admin email. You can answer general questions and use your dummy org as an example. For anything org-specific — they must reach out from an associated admin email.

Hot Leads Process
Identifying and routing prospects who enter Intercom
Important
What Is a Hot Lead?
Any prospect who: enters Intercom with interest in CourtReserve, asks if the product is right for them, asks about specific features, or asks for a demo.
Immediate Action
1. Grab the Intercom ticket URL
2. Post it in the #hot-leads Slack channel immediately
3. Ensure contact info (email, phone) is visible in the ticket

An Account Executive will immediately reach out and start the customer journey.
If No Contact Info
Send the "Hot Leads Send to Sales Team" macro. Edit to avoid sounding canned. It's okay to answer questions and let them know the Sales Team will follow up.
Follow-Up & Closeout
Snooze the ticket after sending the macro. If no response after 2 business days — send a polite reminder, then close the ticket.

Call Handling & Grasshopper
Scheduled calls, Q&A calls, Grasshopper voicemails, and last-minute calls
Important
Scheduled Calls (Q&A, Subscription)
• You'll receive an email when scheduled. Details also posted in #subscription-calls or #qa-calls
• Before: review notes and familiarize yourself with the account
• During: Fathom recorder starts when attendees join. Send recording to all attendees after
• After: update Close with call summary + Fathom link. Open a new Intercom ticket for any promised follow-up (email option, not CR Gmail)
Q&A Call Requests
Ask the customer to provide a list of questions in advance — helps connect them with the right team member. If a call is scheduled, assign the ticket to the Upcoming Q&A Calls view inbox.
Q&A Technical Call Requests
Request in #tsa-call-requests Slack. Must be approved by a TSA first. Write up notes as if it were a bug. Client must provide: bullet list of questions, report filters (if applicable), and available time slots.
Grasshopper Voicemails
Must be approved by Team Lead before handling.
1. Look up the number in Close first
2. If no match — listen to voicemail for context
3. If no identifiable voicemail — call back through Close
4. Determine: player or admin? Handle accordingly
5. If call exceeds 5–10 minutes — let them know you'll follow up via email
6. Leave notes in ticket. Tag with "Grasshopper" tag. Close the ticket.
Last-Minute Calls
When an admin isn't understanding directions in Intercom, you can offer a quick call to walk them through the steps.

Bug Escalation & Dev Tasks
Critical bugs, dev task requests, mass voids, bot detection
Must Know
Critical Bug Rule
An error message is ONLY a critical bug if you can reproduce it. Once confirmed: tag as critical bug immediately — automated notification goes to Nic and Tim. If the bug tag automation is down, write up the bug and assign the ticket directly to Nic.
Dev Tasks
A dev task is something that cannot be done via the user interface. If front-end tools exist to complete it (e.g., mass update), CS team handles it — not dev.
Mass Voids
1. Log into Imports 2 at imports2.courtreserve.com
2. Open Transactions
3. Filter to desired transactions
4. Confirm filters → Select All → Click Void
Bot Detection
We do not tag bot detection requests (exceptions for very large clients with small lists — must be escalated to Team Lead first). Dev is building an Audit Log for automated requests. A reservation randomizer is also available — when enabled, all reservation requests within a defined window (e.g., first 60 seconds after opening) are processed in random order.

Imports & Mass Updates
Member import process, multi-location handling, mass update requests
Important
Initial Handling
Prioritize imports immediately upon receipt. Send the "import received" macro and reassign to the Imports Inbox.
Processing
Review the import. Send a Loom video for updates if needed. Set status to 'away' when working on the import. Inform any active customer you're stepping away and offer to transfer or return shortly.
Multiple Imports & Fees
Organizations get one free import. For multiple locations (Enterprise), one free import per location. Additional imports: $150 base fee + $150/hour. Consult Jeff or Jill on whether to charge.
Post-Import Info to Provide
Post in the Import Channel once complete:
• Import Status: Complete
• Location Name
• Admin Name
• Admin Email

Branded Mobile App (BMA)
BMA requests, status checks, changes, and analytics access
Reference
BMA Request Process
1. Send the BMA Contract Request form to the customer
2. Customer fills out form → automatically receives BMA Contract via email
3. Once signed, notification appears in #cs-notifications channel
4. Team Lead enables "Branded App Agreement Signed" checkbox in SuperAdmin and marks ✅
5. Team Lead tags the AE to send the post-contract setup template in Close
6. Once the Monday form is submitted for dev — status shows as REQUESTED
Status Checks
Ask in CS Questions — a Team Lead will check the branded-mobile-app Slack channel for updates. Dev team notes are in the comments of the request in that channel.
Changes to Published BMA
Tag as Dev Task. Provide: Org/Enterprise Name, Org/Enterprise ID, full name and email of requesting admin, and detailed instructions.
Analytics Access
The admin who requested the BMA has app store analytics access by default. Additional dashboard access for other admins requires a Dev Task with full name, email, and org details.

Payments, Unlocks & Special Billing
Payment provider unlocks, invoicing policy, RSPA discount, CardConnect
Must Know
Payment Provider Unlocks
Always contact Team Lead immediately. Send "Switching Payment Providers" macro and place in #csleads-payments-organization-changes Slack channel. A Team Lead takes over.
Invoicing Policy
We do not do invoicing, annual payments, or purchase orders. We only accept credit cards and eChecks, month to month. Legacy clients on fixed yearly contracts are grandfathered in — we will NOT offer new yearly payments to new clients.
RSPA Discount
We offer a 5% discount through our RSPA partnership. Only offer if asked directly — RSPA informs their members. Notify Ashley directly when someone requests this discount.
CardConnect
Only offer CardConnect when a customer asks about it directly. They are PCI Compliant and also go by: FiServ, First Data, and Cardpoint.

Shifts, Breaks & Handoffs
East coast shift end, west coast shift end, weekend coverage, and lunch rules
Important
Breaks
Quick breaks (stretch, coffee, walk the dog): notify team in Watercooler when you leave and when you return. For lunches and other breaks — always give the customer the option: "I have to step away for 30 minutes. Would you prefer to be transferred to another CS rep, or continue with me when I return?"
East Coast Shift End (4:50 PM Grace Period)
• Set Away status at 4:50 PM
• Wrap up live chats or leave detailed notes for follow-up
• If you finish early — jump in to help teammates with active conversations
• If a ticket is almost resolved — take the extra few minutes to finish it for the customer and the West Coast Team
• Assign unresolved tickets to CS Team Inbox
West Coast Shift End
Resolve as many tickets as possible. For harder tickets — let them know the team will be with them in the morning. For unresolved tickets: summarize research at the bottom, assign to CS Inbox. Post questions in CS Questions with the ticket URL.
Weekend Coverage
• 9–5 coverage
• 12–8 coverage
• Set away and reassign replies when leaving
Lunch
30 minutes only. Cannot accrue and request off later. Update Slack status to show you are at lunch. If a conversation goes long — notify your team lead.

PTO, Flex Time & Holiday Policy
Vacation accrual, sick days, flex time, and advance notice requirements
Reference
PTO Rules
• Paid vacation may roll over up to 40 hours, max accrual of 168 hours/year
• Resets on employment anniversary date
• Cannot be used alongside extra gifted holiday time
• 1 week advance notice for requests of 2 days or less
• 3 weeks advance notice for requests over 2 days
• Sick days require PTO — notify your direct report and request in Paylocity
• All PTO must be requested in Paylocity
Earning Flex Time
Work extra hours when coverage is needed (someone sick, weekend shifts, west coast hours). Volunteer to your direct report — first come, first serve. Flex hours are added to your account.
PTO — Setting Away in Intercom
Set yourself as away and reassign replies. Leave the latest solution note at the bottom of any active conversations so teammates know where you left off.

Meetings & Training
Weekly meetings, 1:1s, quarterly meetings, CS Office Hours, webinars
Reference
Weekly Team Meeting
Wednesdays 12–1PM. Listen and don't interrupt. Be on time. Hold questions until asked. Don't work on other tasks during the meeting. A team lead sets up Intercom messaging before and after.
Bi-Weekly 1:1s
Talk to your leader about how things are going, problem solving, and your development. Good questions to ask: "How am I doing?" and "Is there anything I can be doing better?"
CS Office Hours
Purpose: team training, new hire onboarding, and product expertise. Open to all teams. Technical Specialist announces the weekly training topic. Recordings posted in the team leads' Fathom channel and the training spreadsheet.
Webinars
Monthly "What's New in CourtReserve" webinars. Recordings available on the CourtReserve YouTube channel. You may be asked to participate or present.
Maintaining Your Test Account
Your sandbox should always be built out and ready for demos and webinars. When new features are released — set up and understand the use case. Have all additional features turned on.
